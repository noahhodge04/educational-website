The Basics of For the King

The Genre

For the King is a series of turn-based, tabletop-style, "roguelite" video games. This means that the games
  • resemble TTRPG such as Dungeons and Dragons, Apocalypse World, and Cyberpunk (in some ways)
  • handle combat, exploration, etc. in sequential turns
  • are made to be difficult, replayable, and otherwise designed for multiple runs
  • have runs unlock things outside of the individual run to help in future runs

The Setting

Set in the fantasy world of Fahrul, each game's stories revolve around ordinary townsfolk embarking on an adventure to fight the evil forces of Chaos. The games have a lighthearted tone, making plenty of subtle jokes and references without detracting from the fantasy setting or the stakes of the missions. All manner of standard fantasy foes, especially the likes of goblins, slimess, and the undead, can be found for the player characters to fight.

For the King

For the King happens in the wake of the murder of the good King Bronner and the disappearance of the royal wizard, Vexor. Chaos is rising, and average townsfolk are risking their lives to save the world. This game features 7 decently long campaigns, each one a contained story of its own:
  • For the King, in which Queen Rosomon sends out adventurers to fight the Chaos
  • Dungeon Crawl, a mode dedicated to finding and completing the game's dungeons
  • Frost Adventure, in which adventurers go to the frozen North to find a great tresure
  • Hildebrant's Cellar, an endless dungeon crawl in which players descend ever deeper into a single, infinite dungeon
  • Gold Rush, a competitive mode in which players compete against one another to gather gold for Lucky the Leprechaun
  • Into the Deep, a story where adventurers take to the sea to stop the Sea King from flooding the world
  • Lost Civilization, a DLC story where adventurers travel to the jungles of Fahrul to stop a Chaos cult
